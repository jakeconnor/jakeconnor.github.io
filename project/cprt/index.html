Carleton Planetary Robotics Team

 

The Carleton Planetary Robotics Team is entering its 5th year, and I’m proud to have been a member since its founding. The team’s primary goal is to build rovers for fun, and to compete at competitions like the University Rover Challenge, UK University Rover Challenge, and European Rover Challenge.

This year we traveled to Drumheller, Alberta to compete at the Canadian International Rover Challenge. The team took 2nd place, missing first by a fraction of a percentage point.

This is the team’s second rover, the first of which we took to Manchester, UK, earning a 4th place finish.

I was the electronics lead of the team for 3 years, and now having graduated, I act as an advisor to the team. During my tenure, I designed the power architecture, link budget, wireless communications structure, and many customs PCBs. I managed 15 members of the electronics team split into 5 specialties, interfaced with the mechanical and software teams. I produced financial and mass budgets, managed purchasing, and negotiated sponsorships for the team.

In the time since the team’s founding, we’ve grown from a few space-interested students to more than 40 members, having competed in multiple international competitions, and become one of the leading student teams at Carleton.
